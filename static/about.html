How to Play
1. Start a new game from the main screen 2. Outfit your wagon with food, ammunition, clothing, and supplies 3. Each turn, decide whether to hunt, stop at a fort, or continue 4. Choose your eating rations (affects health and food consumption) 5. Reach Oregon City before running out of resources Combat: When you need to use your rifle, respond as quickly as possible to hit your target. Faster responses = better aim!
Resources
ANIMALS: Your oxen team (spend $200-$300 for better animals = faster travel) FOOD: Essential for survival (more food = less chance of illness) AMMUNITION: Buy in $1 increments (each = 50 bullets for hunting and defense) CLOTHING: Important for cold weather in the mountains SUPPLIES: Includes medicine and emergency repair materials
Tips for Success
- Don't overspend early; save cash for forts - Hunt when you have low food (requires bullets) - Eat moderately to balance food consumption and health - Stock up on supplies before entering the mountains - Good hunting accuracy scores give more food - Manage your clothing carefully for cold weather events
Original Game
The original Oregon Trail was created by Don Rawitsch, Bill Heinemann, and Paul Dillenberger in 1985. It became one of the most popular educational games of all time. This JavaScript version maintains the core gameplay while adapting the interface for modern web and e-reader devices.
